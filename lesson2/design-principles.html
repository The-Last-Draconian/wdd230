Three Graphic Design Principles

By Abigail Taylor

Visual Hierarchy

Visual hierarchy allows the viewer to follow a 'trail' through a design, from the most important to the least. Visual hierarchy can be achieved by utilizing bright colors, heavy and light fonts, large shapes, and leading lines. Wordpress uses a large, bright heading and smaller paragraphs to create visual hierarchy.

Rule of Thirds

The rule of thirds helps break up images and designs into a pleasing structure. When you divide an image or design into thirds (three columns and three rows), where the lines intersect is a pleasing focal point. This is why sometimes and off-set focal point is more aesthetically pleasing than a centered focal point. In this example, Albuquerque Overdrive uses the rule of thirds to position its reading recommendations.

PARC: Repetition

Repetition is all about consistent design. Repeating colors, fonts, styles, and shapes help to pull a design together into a coherent whole. In this example, Help Me Grow repeats the same blues, reds, and font styles to make their website feel consistent.
